Chamber Purpose

Before descending further into the vault, all personnel must undergo complete biological decontamination. This isn't paranoia—it's necessity. The artifacts stored below are so valuable that even microscopic contamination could be catastrophic.

The chamber uses a proprietary tri-phase purification system: UV sterilization, electrostatic particle removal, and what the engineers call "aggressive molecular scrubbing." The process takes exactly 4 minutes and 37 seconds.

The Decontamination Protocol

Standard Operating Procedure

Reference: NW-VAULT-SOP-003

  1. Personal Effects Deposit: All items must be placed in the antimicrobial storage units. This includes clothing, accessories, electronic devices, and any organic materials.
  2. Suit Up: Don the provided sterile chamber suit. The suit is single-use and will be incinerated after your visit. Cost: 847 NX equivalent per suit.
  3. Phase 1 - UV Bath: Stand in the central column while ultraviolet arrays eliminate surface contaminants. Keep eyes closed. Duration: 90 seconds.
  4. Phase 2 - Particle Removal: Electrostatic fields strip away microscopic debris. You may feel a slight tingling sensation. This is normal. Duration: 120 seconds.
  5. Phase 3 - Molecular Scrub: A proprietary mist neutralizes remaining biological agents. Breathe normally. The mist is harmless (we think). Duration: 67 seconds.
  6. Clearance: Green light indicates successful decontamination. Proceed to elevator B4. Red light indicates failure—restart protocol.

Note: Average failure rate is 2.3%. Common causes include residual cologne, artificial food additives, and "that weird thing nobody talks about from 2019."

The 2023 Incident

DECLASSIFIED: INCIDENT REPORT #B3-2023-07

On March 17, 2023, a senior guild member bypassed Phase 2 of the decontamination protocol, claiming "time constraints." Within 12 hours, a microscopic dust particle from their keyboard had traveled through the ventilation system to floor B11.

The particle—measuring 0.003mm—landed on the edge of a display case containing a first-edition guild charter. Automated systems detected the contamination at 3:47 AM and initiated emergency lockdown.

The charter required 847 hours of restoration work. The member in question now voluntarily undergoes quadruple decontamination. Every. Single. Time.

Lesson: The protocol exists for a reason. Don't be that person.
