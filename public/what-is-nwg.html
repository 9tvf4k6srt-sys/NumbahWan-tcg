What is NWG?

A plain-language guide to NumbahWan's in-game currency — what it does, how it works, and what to know before you spend money on it.

The Short Version

NWG (NumbahWan Gold) is the premium currency inside the NumbahWan guild. You use it to open card packs, buy merch, stake for rewards, and trade with other members. Think of it like credits in a game — except with a few extra mechanics built in.

The fixed exchange rate is $1 USD = 100 NWG. There will only ever be 1 billion NWG. No more can be created.

Why Convert NWG to Gold?

The exchange rate is 10 NWG = 1 Gold. Here's the honest breakdown of when it makes sense and when it doesn't:

When it makes sense
  • You want guaranteed cards. Gold pulls let you bypass RNG. If you need a specific rarity to complete a set, it's the fastest path.
  • You're building through fusion. Legendary fusion costs 10,000 Gold. Earning that amount purely from gameplay takes time — converting NWG shortcuts it.
  • You're an active arcade player. If you're good at the games, Gold spent in the arcade can multiply — including into Sacred Logs.
When it doesn't make sense
  • It's one-way. Once you convert NWG to Gold, you can never convert Gold back. This is a permanent decision.
  • NWG has direct uses. The NWG Shop, premium merch, staking, and marketplace all use NWG directly. Converting too much to Gold leaves you short for those features.
  • 2% burn penalty. Converting 100 NWG to Gold costs 2 NWG in burns. So 100 NWG only gets you 980 Gold, not 1,000.
  • Gold can be earned for free. If you're patient, you can accumulate Gold through daily play without spending NWG at all.
The real incentive

Gold's purpose is to be the gameplay loop currency. NWG is the economy backbone. Think of it this way: NWG is your savings account, Gold is your spending cash for the game itself. The system is designed so that active players earn Gold naturally, while NWG conversion is a shortcut for people who value time over grinding.

The honest answer: you don't need to buy NWG to get Gold. But if you want to skip the grind for fusions and guaranteed pulls, converting NWG is the fast lane. That's the real trade-off.

How Burns Work

Every time NWG changes hands, a small percentage is permanently destroyed ("burned"). This means the total supply slowly shrinks over time.

Action Burn Rate What It Means
Any NWG Transaction 1% Spend 100 NWG, 1 is burned
NWG → Gold Exchange 2% Higher burn for converting out
Transfers Between Users 0.5% Small cost to send NWG to others
Why it matters

Burns are deflationary — the more the economy is used, the fewer NWG exist. In theory, this creates scarcity over time. But it only matters if people actually use and want NWG. Activity drives the effect, not just the mechanic.

How staking rewards are paid

Rewards come from the rewards pool (30% of total supply set aside for this). When you stake, your NWG is locked — you can't spend or transfer it until the period ends. Early unstaking incurs a 10% penalty.

You can also stake cards (up to 5 at a time). Cards earn NWG daily based on their rarity and star level. Higher-rarity cards generate more, but they're also harder to pull.

What Backs NWG?

NWG's value comes from a few different sources. None of these are a guarantee — they're the mechanics the system is built on:

  • Fixed supply: 1 billion NWG max. No new tokens can be created. Burns reduce this over time.
  • Utility: NWG is needed for card packs, premium merch, staking, and trading. It has uses inside the guild.
  • Revenue backing: 10% of merch revenue is used to buy back NWG, supporting the price floor.
  • Asset tracking: The NWG price on our /markets page tracks a basket of real-world assets (Silver, Gold, Bitcoin, Palantir, Broadcom) to give it a reference value.
Being honest about this

NWG is not a traditional cryptocurrency on a public blockchain. It's an internal guild currency. Its value is tied to the guild's activity and growth. There is currently no external exchange where you can sell NWG for USD. This may change in the future, but right now, NWG has value within the NumbahWan ecosystem.

Things To Know Before Buying

Risks & Limitations
  • No external marketplace yet. You can't currently sell NWG back to USD on an exchange. Liquidity is limited to in-game uses.
  • Early-stage project. NumbahWan is new. The economy, staking rates, and mechanics may change as the project evolves.
  • Value depends on adoption. Burns and scarcity only help if there's real demand. If fewer people use NWG, the mechanics alone don't create value.
  • Staking locks your funds. Locked NWG can't be used until the period ends. Early withdrawal costs 10%.
  • This is not financial advice. We're sharing how the system works so you can make your own decision.

Common Questions

Is NWG a cryptocurrency?

Not in the traditional sense. It's an internal digital currency for the NumbahWan guild. It uses some crypto-inspired mechanics (fixed supply, burns, staking) but it's not on a public blockchain.

Can I lose money?

If you buy NWG expecting to sell it for more USD later — there's no guarantee of that. Right now there's no external market. Only buy what you're comfortable spending on in-game items and experiences.

What happens to burned NWG?

It's gone permanently. Burned NWG is subtracted from the total supply and can never be recovered. You can see the current burn total on the invest page.

Can I earn NWG for free?

Yes. You get 100 NWG when you sign up as a citizen, plus daily login rewards (up to 70 NWG/day on a 7-day streak), referral bonuses (100 NWG each), and staking yields. Many members never buy NWG directly.

What makes NWG different from other game currencies?

Two things: the supply actually decreases over time (burns), and there are staking mechanics that reward holding. Most game currencies inflate — NWG is designed to do the opposite. Whether that makes it a good deal depends on how the guild grows.

Should I convert all my NWG to Gold?

No. Gold is only useful for fusion, guaranteed pulls, and arcade. NWG is needed for the shop, merch, staking, and premium features. Convert only what you plan to use in gameplay. Think of Gold as spending cash and NWG as your long-term balance.

Can I convert Gold back to NWG?

No. The NWG → Gold conversion is one-way and permanent. Once converted, that Gold can only be spent on gameplay features. This is by design to preserve NWG scarcity.

Where can I see live stats?

The invest page has a live burn tracker and supply dashboard. The /markets page shows real-time backing asset prices.
