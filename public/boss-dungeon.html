LORE

The Fall of the Guild Master

Nobody saw it coming. RegginA — the benevolent dictator who read one chapter of a leadership book and got distracted by a gacha banner — had always been chaotic. But corruption? The guild master who once kept 200 people in a boss fight for 6 hours because she was "sure we could get it next try" had finally snapped.

The trigger was simple: someone out-whaled her. A rival guild leader spent $51,000 on a single banner — exactly $1,000 more than RegginA's legendary Whale Wars spending spree. The humiliation was total. The corruption was immediate. The credit card bill? Catastrophic.

Consumed by the Corrupted Crown of Infinite Spending, RegginA transformed into a colossal fire deity — The Eternal Flame. Her power scales with every dollar ever spent on gacha. That's approximately 2 billion HP worth of poor financial decisions.

THE DUNGEON

The Eternal Throne: 7 Floors of Chaos

The dungeon descends 7 floors beneath Castle NumbahWan, each themed after a chapter of RegginA's reign. From the Gauntlet of Flame at the entrance to The Eternal Throne room at the deepest level, every floor introduces new mechanics that require all 2,000 players to work in perfect sync.

BOSS PHASES

7 Phases of Absolute Madness

Each phase unlocks after the previous one is cleared. The boss heals between phases during rest periods, so every shift must push progress. The raid takes 7–14 real-world days because this boss doesn't believe in speed runs — and neither did RegginA.

PHASE 1 — FLOOR 1
The Gauntlet of Flame
Tank Rotation Fire Wave Dodge Shield Wall Sync

The boss opens with massive sweeping flame attacks. All 400 tanks must form a rotating shield wall — groups of 50 swap every 90 seconds. If one group is late, the entire front line gets one-shot by "Whale's Judgment." Meanwhile DPS must dodge fire waves that sweep in patterns.

It's like a synchronized DPS test where 2,000 people must hit their damage window at the exact same time. Gold coins rain from the ceiling as the boss taunts your spending habits.

PHASE 3 — FLOOR 3
The Shadow Realm
Position Puzzle Rune Formation 2000-Player Sync

The arena splits into light and dark halves. Players must stand on specific nodes to form a giant rune pattern visible only from above. Each of the 2,000 players has an assigned position. One person in the wrong spot breaks the entire rune. It's essentially a 2,000-piece human jigsaw puzzle where the pieces have opinions.

PHASE 4 — FLOOR 4
The Crystal Gauntlet
Relay Race Ice Corridor Timed Activation

Each squad has 4 minutes. The corridors have sliding ice platforms, frost traps, and a mini-boss — the Crystal Sentinel — guarding the final pillar.

The Final Stand
Unity Beam Enrage Timer Redemption Window

MECHANICS

Signature Raid Mechanics

Beyond the 7 phases, the dungeon features persistent mechanics that run throughout the entire multi-week raid. Master these or perish — there are no shortcuts when fighting someone who spent $50,000 on a mobile game.

🌙 The 3 AM Test

Twice per raid, the boss enters a special "Nocturnal Rage" mode that can only be triggered between 2:00–4:00 AM server time. During this window, she becomes 40% weaker — but only the bravest (or most sleep-deprived) players can take advantage. This is a direct callback to RegginA's infamous 3 AM raid call that 147 guild members actually showed up for.

MECHANIC
The 3 AM Test — exhausted players with coffee emotes, Zzz effects, and a boss who taunts sleepy raiders
Fun Fact: During playtesting, developers actually scheduled the 3 AM test at 3 AM. 80% of the QA team slept through it. The bug report read: "Feature working as intended."
💀 The Wipe Protocol

When a raid wipe occurs, all 2,000 players die simultaneously in a spectacular cascade of explosions. The boss performs a victory emote — she pulls out a credit card and swipes it dramatically. Players respawn at the last save beacon and must reorganize all 8 battalions before the next attempt. Average reorganization time: 45 minutes of controlled chaos.

WIPE SCREEN
Total raid wipe — the aftermath of a failed Unity Beam, all 2,000 players down simultaneously
✨ The Resurrection Engine

A dedicated resurrection squad of 60 holy priests operates in rotating pairs behind the battle lines. When players fall, the res squad has 15 seconds to revive them before the soul dissipates. With 2,000 players, death happens constantly — the resurrection squad processes an average of 200 revives per hour. It's basically an ER ward powered by prayer and energy drinks.

REST SYSTEM

Designed for Real Life: The Shift System

We know what you're thinking: "A raid that lasts WEEKS? I have a job." Don't worry — the dungeon is designed with real-world sustainability in mind. The Eternal Throne features a mandatory shift system that ensures no player needs to sacrifice their life, job, or sanity. Well, maybe a little sanity.

SAFE ZONE
The Rest Camp — cozy safe zone inside the dungeon with shift schedule board, campfire, food buffs, and bedrolls
SHIFT TIME (SERVER) ACTIVE PLAYERS NOTES
☀️ Morning 06:00 – 14:00 ~700 players Focus on phase progression & steady damage
🌅 Afternoon 14:00 – 22:00 ~800 players (peak) Prime time — most phase transitions happen here
🌙 Night 22:00 – 06:00 ~500 players Maintenance & chip damage. 3 AM Test window opens here.
Key Design Principles:
  • 🔄 Rotating Roster: Each player commits to 2–3 shifts per week (4–8 hours each). Nobody needs to be online 24/7.
  • 💾 Progress Saves: Boss HP doesn't reset between shifts. Every point of damage counts, no matter when it's dealt.
  • 🛏️ AFK Protection: If your shift replacement arrives, you can log off guilt-free. The boss isn't going anywhere. She lives here now.
  • ⏰ Catch-Up Buffs: Players who missed a day get a temporary damage boost so they're never permanently behind. RegginA hated AFK members, but we don't.
  • 🏕️ Rest Camps: Safe zones inside the dungeon with campfires that grant food buffs, repair stations, and a cozy atmosphere. You can chill here between shifts.
"The dungeon may take weeks, but YOUR commitment is just a few hours at a time. Think of it like a really intense book club where the book fights back." — Lead Game Designer
