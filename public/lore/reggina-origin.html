Chapter 1

Day Zero

Every legend has a beginning. For RegginA, it started on a random Tuesday at 2:47 AM, when sleep deprivation and questionable life choices collided with a mobile game download button.

"Just one game," she told herself. "Just to see what all the fuss is about."

Three years later, she would become the most powerful guild master on the Taiwan server, commanding an army of degenerates, whales, and grinders who would follow her into any raid, any battle, any absolutely terrible decision.

But we're getting ahead of ourselves.

"I didn't choose the grind life. The grind life saw my credit card and chose me."

The Name

"RegginA" wasn't her first choice. Her original username was xXFlameGirlXx, but it was taken. So was FlameQueen, FireMaster, and literally every fire-related name she could think of.

In a fit of 3 AM desperation, she mashed her keyboard. The result? A name that would become legendary... and also cause endless confusion in guild chat.

Chapter 2

The Rise of a Guild Master

Most players join guilds. RegginA built one.

"NumbahWan" started as a joke. Three friends with a shared love of grinding and a complete disregard for sleep schedules. The name was supposed to be ironic—they were ranked #847 on the server.

Six months later, they weren't joking anymore.

Chapter 3

Leadership Style: Controlled Chaos

Ask any NumbahWan member what it's like to be led by RegginA, and you'll get a different answer every time.

"She's like a strict parent who also enables all your worst impulses," says Natehouoho, the guild's top grinder. "One minute she's telling you to get some sleep, the next she's organizing a 4 AM raid."

"She remembers everyone's birthday," adds RegginO, the Vice Master. "But also their most embarrassing in-game moments. And she will bring them up."

Chapter 4

Why "The Eternal Flame"?

Every mythic card has a title. RegginA's is "The Eternal Flame".

The title wasn't chosen for its poetry (though it is poetic). It was earned through one specific incident that became guild legend.

During the Great Server Merge of 2024, when half the guilds dissolved and the other half were absorbed by mega-guilds, NumbahWan was targeted for hostile takeover. Three top-100 guilds coordinated an attack on their territory.

RegginA's response? She organized a defense that lasted 72 hours straight. She didn't sleep. She barely ate. She burned through resources like they were unlimited.

When asked why she didn't just surrender, her answer became the guild motto:

"Because NumbahWan doesn't go out. We burn forever."
— RegginA, Hour 71 of the Great Defense

The 73rd Hour

What RegginA never told anyone: she collapsed at hour 73. Her phone fell from her hand as she passed out from exhaustion.

When she woke up 8 hours later, she found that RegginO had taken over command seamlessly. The defense held. The guild survived.

It was the day she truly understood what "guild" meant.

Chapter 5

The RegginA Philosophy

Over the years, RegginA has developed a set of principles that guide NumbahWan. They're not written down anywhere official, but every member knows them by heart.

"People ask me why NumbahWan works when so many other guilds fall apart," RegginA once explained. "The answer is simple: we're not a guild. We're a family of idiots who happen to be really good at video games."

What Comes Next?

RegginA doesn't talk much about the future. When pressed, she usually deflects with a joke or changes the subject.

But late at night, in the quiet hours between raids, she sometimes hints at a bigger vision. Something beyond guild rankings and server dominance.

The Sacred Log

There's a reason RegginA founded NumbahWan. A reason she's been grinding for three years straight. A reason she named the guild's most precious artifact "The Sacred Log."

On her first day playing, she received a random in-game item: a wooden log. Worthless by any gameplay standard. But the description read:

"A simple log. Worthless to most. But to someone, somewhere, it might mean everything."

She never sold it. Never will. It reminds her that value isn't about stats or rarity—it's about meaning.

That's what NumbahWan is built on. Not power. Purpose.
