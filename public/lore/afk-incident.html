The Great AFK Incident

When half the guild disappeared during the most important raid in history.

Chapter 1: The Perfect Storm

December 14th, 2022. The date that will live in infamy. NumbahWan had been preparing for The Siege of Zakum's Gate for three weeks. A server-first opportunity. The entire guild had promised to be there. Attendance was "mandatory" (air quotes intentional).

The raid was scheduled for 8:00 PM server time—chosen carefully to accommodate players across multiple time zones. RegginA had sent 47 reminders. The strategy guide was 23 pages long. Everything was perfect.

At 7:59 PM, 156 guild members were online. At 8:01 PM, only 73 remained. Half the guild had gone AFK simultaneously.

Chapter 2: The Hall of Excuses

Over the following 24 hours, guild leadership received 83 excuses for the mass AFK. These have been meticulously archived and ranked by the HR Department:

  • #1 "My cat walked across my keyboard and somehow logged me out and then deleted the game." Used by: 1 person
  • #2 "I forgot about the raid because I was watching a documentary about forgetting things." Used by: 2 people
  • #3 "My mom made dinner. Yes, I'm 34." Used by: 7 people
  • #4 "I thought the raid was tomorrow. I also thought today was Wednesday. It was Friday." Used by: 12 people
  • #5 "I was there in spirit. My character was literally standing in the raid zone." Used by: 1 person (technically true)
  • #6 "Power outage." (Verified: 3 legitimate, 15 suspicious) Used by: 18 people
  • #7 "I had to go to work. At 8 PM. On a Saturday." Used by: 4 people
  • #8 "I was there but invisible. Check the server logs." (The server logs showed they were in the tutorial zone.) Used by: 1 genius
SECRET: The REAL Reason (According to Investigation)
Click to discover what actually happened...
A forensic analysis of the guild Discord server revealed a suspicious message sent at 7:58 PM: "lol who's actually going to this raid tho." This message triggered a cascade of "actually I might skip too" responses. The phenomenon, now studied by guild psychologists, has been dubbed "The Cascade of Apathy." The original message sender has never been identified. Some say they're still among us.

Chapter 3: The Attempted Raid

With only 73 members present (the minimum recommended was 120), RegginA faced a choice: cancel and admit defeat, or attempt the impossible. In a moment that perfectly encapsulates NumbahWan's philosophy, they chose violence.

"We're going in. If we die, we die. If we somehow win, I want every single AFK member to know they missed the greatest achievement in guild history. Either way, I win." — RegginA, right before the wipe

The raid lasted exactly 4 minutes and 37 seconds. The guild wiped on the first mini-boss—a creature that, with full attendance, should have taken approximately 45 seconds to defeat. The damage logs showed that 12 of the 73 present members were also AFK but had at least remembered to enter the raid zone.

RegginA's post-wipe speech lasted longer than the raid itself. It has been transcribed, edited for content, and is now required reading for all new guild members.

Chapter 4: The Great Purge (That Didn't Happen)

In the aftermath, RegginA threatened to kick every AFK member. A vote was held. The vote failed because half the members eligible to vote were the AFK members. Democracy has never felt so cruel.

Instead, a new system was implemented: the "Attendance Shame Board"—a public display showing each member's raid attendance percentage. Members below 50% would have their names displayed in a special gray font in guild chat. This punishment was considered "devastating" by exactly zero people.

FINAL SECRET: The Redemption Arc
Click to learn how this story ends...
Two months later, NumbahWan attempted The Siege of Zakum's Gate again. This time, RegginA scheduled it for 3:00 AM to "weed out the casuals." 147 members showed up. They achieved server-second (another guild had beaten it by 6 hours). When asked if the 3 AM time was worth it, RegginA responded: "The server-first guild had better attendance. We had better vibes." They then fell asleep mid-interview.
