Chapter 1: The Spark

It started, as many wars do, over something incredibly petty. During a routine guild chat, WHALE_DADDY_69 casually mentioned that they had "basically carried" the guild to their latest achievement. This did not sit well with xX_SPEND_Xx, who responded with nine words that would echo through NumbahWan history:

"Check the receipts, little fish. I own this pond." — xX_SPEND_Xx, 23:47 UTC, March 14, 2023

The response was immediate. Within 30 minutes, both players had shared their spending histories in what became known as "The Receipt Duel." What followed was a 72-hour spending spree that would test the limits of credit card companies, marriages, and basic human dignity.

Fun fact: The banner they were fighting over was for a character that would be powercrept exactly 6 weeks later. The irony is not lost on anyone.

Chapter 3: The Aftermath

When the dust settled, both players had maxed constellations on the banner character. Both had spent enough money to buy a reasonably decent used car. And both immediately began arguing about who "won."

xX_SPEND_Xx's "supportive" spouse filed for divorce three months later. The legal documents specifically mentioned "the gacha incident."

The guild's response was mixed. Some viewed it as an inspiring display of dedication. Others viewed it as a cautionary tale about what happens when you let competitive personalities loose with credit cards. Most just enjoyed the spectacle.

"I've seen a lot in my time running this guild. Wars. Drama. Betrayals. But watching two grown adults spend a combined year's salary on virtual currency because one called the other a 'little fish'? That's a new one." — RegginA, in a rare moment of lucidity

Chapter 4: The Lesson (There Isn't One)

If you're expecting a profound moral to this story, you've come to the wrong guild. The Whale Wars remain one of the most referenced events in NumbahWan history, brought up whenever anyone considers making a "reasonable" $100 purchase.

Guild Rule #47 (added March 18, 2023): "Spending challenges are prohibited. If you witness a spending challenge developing, contact guild leadership immediately. We will not stop it, but we want to watch."

To this day, the phrase "Check the receipts" is banned from guild chat to prevent a repeat incident. The ban has been violated 847 times.
