Executive Summary

This annual security review catalogues all 847 documented intrusion attempts against the NumbahWan Vault between January 2024 and December 2026. We analyze failure points by floor level, intruder classification, and methodology employed. Key findings: 73% of attempts terminated at B5 (Soul Signature Authentication), 24% at B10 (Crimson Maze laser grid), and 3% were cats who somehow reached B7 before triggering motion sensors. Recommendations for security enhancements are provided, though we note with satisfaction that current systems maintain a perfect 100% prevention rate.

1. Introduction

The NumbahWan Vault, extending 195 meters beneath Parry Hotter Academy's West Wing, houses artifacts of incalculable value. Since the vault's hardened operations began in 2019, security protocols have evolved from "adequate" to what external auditors describe as "pathologically excessive." This report documents and analyzes all recorded intrusion attempts during the 2024-2026 reporting period.

We emphasize at the outset: zero successful breaches occurred during this period. This perfect record continues an unbroken streak dating to the vault's establishment. While some may view this report as unnecessary—why analyze failures when success is assured?—we maintain that understanding threat patterns enables proactive security enhancement.

2.2 The Cat Problem

The 25 feline intrusions merit special discussion. Analysis indicates that cats enter the vault access corridors through ventilation systems designed with 15cm×15cm clearances— sufficient for air circulation but, evidently, also sufficient for determined cats. Three of these cats have since been adopted by vault personnel and now serve as unofficial "security consultants" (their primary duty appears to be sleeping on warm server equipment).

"The cat reached B7. B7. We have seventeen separate authentication checkpoints and a cat just... walked through. We've since added infrared motion sensors calibrated for quadrupedal movement patterns. The cat's name is now 'Security' and he has his own badge."
— Vault Operations Log, Incident #417, September 2024

4.2 GUARDIAN AI Performance Metrics

The GUARDIAN AI system, operational since 2023, processes 2.3 petabytes of surveillance data per second across 847 active camera feeds. During the reporting period, GUARDIAN correctly identified all human intrusion attempts within an average of 0.3 seconds of first visibility. The system's only weakness—a 12.4% false positive rate—stems from its inability to distinguish between genuine threats and: cats, large insects, floating dust illuminated by maintenance lighting, and one memorable instance of a balloon that escaped from a birthday party three floors above.

"GUARDIAN flagged Threat Level 3 for a spider. A small spider. I spent four hours on lockdown while armed response teams searched for an 'eight-legged hostile.' The spider was ultimately neutralized with a napkin. I've since adjusted sensitivity thresholds."
— GUARDIAN Operations Log, 2025-06-14

5. Intruder Outcome Analysis

5.1 Post-Apprehension Protocols

All apprehended intruders undergo assessment to determine appropriate outcome. The guild maintains three standard protocols:

Protocol Application Count (2024-2026)
Standard Memory Erasure (SMEP) Non-hostile intruders, first offense 723 (85.4%)
Enhanced Interrogation + SMEP Suspected guild agents, repeat offenders 96 (11.3%)
Permanent Banishment Hostile actors, documented threats 28 (3.3%)

Memory Erasure protocols target only vault-related memories; subjects retain full cognitive function and personal memories. Post-SMEP monitoring indicates 94% of subjects experience mild confusion ("I feel like I forgot something important") that dissipates within 72 hours.

5.2 Recidivism Rate

Of the 723 subjects who underwent SMEP, 12 (1.7%) subsequently attempted repeat intrusion. This remarkably low recidivism rate validates SMEP efficacy, though it raises philosophical questions about free will that the Security Division declines to engage with.

6. Recommendations

6.1 Proposed Enhancements

Recommendation 1: Install cat-specific deterrents in HVAC access points. Proposed solutions include ultrasonic emitters (rejected—cruel to cats), smaller vents (rejected—fire code violations), and strategically placed cucumber vegetables (under testing).

Recommendation 2: Investigate B5 bypass method used in Incident #156. Current hypothesis: insider assistance. All personnel with B5 access have undergone enhanced loyalty verification. Two resignations resulted.

Recommendation 3: Adjust GUARDIAN AI sensitivity to reduce false positives while maintaining detection capability. Proposed: machine learning training set expansion to include 10,000+ images of cats, spiders, and party balloons.

7. Conclusion

The NumbahWan Vault security apparatus continues to perform at exceptional levels. With 847 intrusion attempts and zero successful breaches, we maintain our perfect record. The diversity of threat actors—from professional thieves to confused cats—demonstrates both the vault's reputation and its universal impermeability.

We close with a reminder to all personnel: security is not merely a system, but a culture. The vault's contents—particularly Artifact #001—represent the guild's heritage, identity, and sacred trust. Every attempted breach, no matter how amateur, reinforces our resolve.

And to any readers contemplating intrusion: we have a wall of 847 failure cases. We have laser grids that reconfigure faster than you can move. We have an AI that processes more data per second than your brain processes in a year. We have a cat named Security who has more vault access than you ever will.

Choose wisely.
