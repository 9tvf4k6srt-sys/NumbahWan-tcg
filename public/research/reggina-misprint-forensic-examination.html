The diagonal tear on Misprint #001 (the "RegginA" card) measures exactly 47.3mm in length at an angle of 23.7° from horizontal. This paper presents forensic analysis of the tear pattern, examining whether the damage was accidental—as officially recorded—or deliberate. Through fiber pattern analysis, stress mark examination, and geometric modeling, we present evidence that the tear bisects the flame artwork with mathematical precision unlikely to occur through random mechanical failure. We propose three hypotheses for the tear's origin, though final conclusions remain classified by order of the Founding Council.

1. Introduction

The RegginA Misprint, designated Artifact #001 in the NumbahWan Vault registry, represents the most valuable single item in the guild's collection—and possibly in the broader MapleStory memorabilia market. The card's fame stems from a production error that printed the name "Regina" as "RegginA" due to a font rendering bug, combined with a misaligned fire element gradient that creates an unintended visual effect of the flames appearing to "move."

What elevates this artifact from merely rare to singularly precious is the diagonal tear across its upper-left corner. According to guild records, this damage occurred during the card's recovery from the incident at [LOCATION REDACTED] in 2009. The official narrative holds that the tear was accidental—a consequence of hasty extraction under hostile conditions.

Yet questions persist. Why does the tear align so precisely with the flame artwork? Why does its angle—23.7°—correspond exactly to the "golden angle" derivative commonly used in aesthetic composition? This paper subjects the tear to forensic scrutiny, seeking answers that have eluded the guild for fifteen years.

2.3 Stress Mark Distribution

When paper tears, stress marks radiate from the initiation point in characteristic patterns. High-speed tears produce narrow, concentrated stress fields; slow, deliberate tears produce broader, diffuse patterns. The RegginA tear exhibits a hybrid pattern: narrow stress concentration at the initiation point (upper-left) transitioning to broader distribution toward termination (lower-right).

This gradient suggests the tear began with sudden force application, then transitioned to controlled propagation. One interpretation: the tear was initiated accidentally but then deliberately continued. An alternative interpretation: this section has been removed by Vault Security.

3. The 23.7° Angle

3.1 Mathematical Significance

The tear angle of 23.7° is not random. This value is precisely 137.5° minus 113.8°—or, expressed differently, 90° minus the golden angle (66.3°) equals 23.7°. The golden angle (approximately 137.508°) appears throughout nature in leaf arrangements, flower petals, and spiral patterns. Its complement, 23.7°, is less commonly discussed but appears in several artistic composition frameworks.

The probability of a random tear occurring at exactly 23.7° (within ±0.3° tolerance) is approximately 1 in 300, assuming uniform angular distribution. Combined with the precise bisection of the flame artwork, the compound probability drops below 1 in 10,000.

4. Hypotheses

4.1 Hypothesis A: Accidental Tear with Coincidental Alignment

The official guild position holds that the tear occurred during recovery operations in 2009 when the card was hastily extracted from [LOCATION]. According to this narrative, extraction team member "Codename: Shadow" caught the card on an obstruction during egress, producing the tear. The geometric alignment with the flame artwork is dismissed as coincidence—improbable but not impossible.

Assessment: This hypothesis is consistent with the tear's initiation pattern (sudden force) but inconsistent with its propagation pattern (controlled continuation). Additionally, the statistical improbability of the alignment remains unexplained.

4.2 Hypothesis B: Deliberate Damage as Authentication

An alternative hypothesis proposes that the tear was deliberately inflicted as an authentication measure. Like intentional "flaws" in Persian rugs or serial number variations in collectibles, a unique, impossible-to-replicate damage pattern would serve as proof of authenticity. Future forgeries, no matter how sophisticated, could never reproduce the exact fiber pattern of the original tear.

Assessment: This hypothesis explains the tear's geometric precision but raises questions about why such extreme measures would be necessary for an already one-of-a-kind artifact. Additionally, no Founding Council member has confirmed this interpretation.

6. Conclusion

The RegginA tear remains, in the final analysis, a mystery. Our forensic examination has revealed patterns inconsistent with purely accidental damage, yet we cannot definitively conclude deliberate intervention. The geometric alignments are too precise to dismiss, yet too inexplicable to accept.
