EXHIBIT 003

The Sauna Robe

A Rite of Passage • Circa 2003

SECTION I

The Noob's Journey

Every MapleStory veteran remembers their first Sauna Robe. Not because it was valuable—it wasn't. Not because it was rare—it was the opposite of rare. They remember it because crafting that simple white bathrobe represented something profound: the moment they stopped being a confused newcomer and began their transformation into a real player.

The Sauna Robe was obtainable through an NPC crafting quest that required players to gather 10 pieces of leather from monsters in Kerning City. For experienced players, this was trivial—perhaps fifteen minutes of casual hunting. For a brand-new character with starter equipment and no understanding of game mechanics, it could take hours. Days, even.

"I spent three days trying to get enough leather for my first Sauna Robe. I kept dying to those Orange Mushrooms. My brother laughed at me. But when I finally put on that robe, I felt like I had accomplished something real. Twenty years later, I still remember that feeling."

— Park Ji-hoon, Original Beta Tester, Seoul

The robe's design was deliberately humble: a plain white garment with minimal detailing. It looked like something you might wear after a bath or a spa session. There was nothing heroic about it. And perhaps that was the point. The Sauna Robe taught players that in MapleStory, you started from nothing. Glory would come later—but first, you had to earn your bathrobe.

SECTION II

The Crafting Ritual

01

The Quest Begins

Speak to NPC "JM From tha Streetz" in Kerning City. He will explain that he needs leather to make clothes for the city's impoverished residents. (He does not mention that you are also impoverished.)

02

The Hunt

Travel to the Construction Site and defeat Octopus monsters. Each Octopus has approximately a 30% chance to drop leather. You will need 10 pieces. Math suggests 33 kills on average. Reality suggests many more.

03

The Return

Present the leather to JM. He will be inexplicably grateful for materials that took you hours to gather but which he could probably buy for 500 mesos.

04

The Transformation

Receive your Sauna Robe. Equip it immediately. Stand in town square. Feel, for the first time, like you belong in this world.

SECTION III

Sociological Significance

"The Sauna Robe functions as what anthropologists call a 'liminal object'—an artifact that marks the transition from one social state to another. Wearing it signaled that you had completed your first trial, that you had learned the basic grammar of the game's systems. It was a diploma, a union card, a bar mitzvah certificate."

— Dr. Michelle Santos, University of California Berkeley, Department of Anthropology

Interestingly, many high-level players kept their original Sauna Robes long after they had acquired vastly superior equipment. Some kept them in storage as mementos. Others would occasionally equip them in town squares, a display of nostalgic humility that communicated: "I remember where I came from. I was once as weak as you."

"The genius of the Sauna Robe was its universality. Unlike boss drops or cash shop items, everyone could get one. It created a shared experience, a common reference point. When strangers met in MapleStory, they could always bond over the memory of getting their first robe."

— Prof. David Kim, Game Design Theory, NYU
