EXHIBIT 005

The 7-Slot Work Gloves

The Holy Grail of Scrolling • 2005

The Mathematics of Desire

To understand the Work Gloves, you must first understand scrolling. In MapleStory's equipment enhancement system, items could be upgraded using scrolls. Each scroll had a success rate—typically 10%, 30%, 60%, or 100%. A failed scroll destroyed an upgrade slot permanently. A successful scroll added stats. The game was, at its heart, a gambling simulator wearing the skin of an adventure game.

Work Gloves were special because they dropped with a variable number of upgrade slots: anywhere from 0 to 7. A 7-slot pair was vanishingly rare—perhaps one in ten thousand drops. But if you found one, and if you successfully scrolled all seven slots with 10% attack scrolls, you would possess one of the most powerful gloves in the entire game. The odds of this happening were approximately 1 in 10 million.

Perfect Scroll Odds

"I watched a player in the Free Market spend 47 billion mesos on 10% scrolls trying to perfect a 7-slot Work Gloves. He failed on the sixth scroll. I remember the silence in the market—everyone had been watching. When he logged off, he never came back. We never heard from him again."

— Testimony from "The Great Scrolling Disasters of 2006" archive

Economic Implications

The Work Gloves became the de facto currency for high-value transactions. When deals exceeded the meso cap (2.147 billion), traders would specify prices in "clean 7-slot Work Gloves." A thriving secondary market emerged around gloves verification, with trusted middlemen charging fees to authenticate slot counts and scroll histories.

"The 7-slot Work Gloves market was MapleStory's first true financial instrument. It had price discovery, liquidity providers, even derivatives—people would sell 'options' on gloves they didn't yet own. We accidentally recreated Wall Street inside a game about mushrooms."

— Dr. Jennifer Walsh, "Virtual Economies and Real Consequences" (2018)
