EXHIBIT 004

Spectrum Goggles

Vision Beyond the Visible • Ultra Rare

The Chromatic Mystery

Of all the face accessories in MapleStory history, none generated more speculation, more desire, or more outright obsession than the Spectrum Goggles. Their rainbow-tinted lenses seemed to shimmer with an otherworldly luminescence, as if the wearer could perceive frequencies of light invisible to ordinary players.

The goggles dropped from a specific monster that spawned only during certain server conditions that were never officially documented. Players spent years attempting to reverse-engineer the drop mechanics. Forum threads running thousands of pages debated spawn timers, moon phases, and player karma systems. None were ever conclusively proven.

"I hunted for the Spectrum Goggles for eight months. Eight months! I learned the spawn patterns of every monster in that zone. I tracked server maintenance schedules. I even kept a spreadsheet of weather conditions in Seoul, thinking maybe the developers tied it to real-world data. When I finally got them, I cried. Actually cried."

— Lee Min-young, Guild "ChromaHunters" Founder

The Color Theory

What made the Spectrum Goggles so visually distinctive was their use of a full rainbow gradient across the lens surface. In the limited palette of early MapleStory, such color complexity was almost unheard of. Most items used 3-4 colors maximum. The goggles used 7, cycling through the entire visible spectrum in a single 16-pixel-wide accessory.

"From a technical standpoint, the Spectrum Goggles shouldn't exist. The sprite limitations of the original engine made rainbow gradients extremely expensive in terms of rendering. Someone at Wizet really wanted these goggles to look special, and they burned significant resources to make it happen."

— Technical analysis by the MapleStory Preservation Society, 2019
