THE MAPLE BANDANA

The Original Anniversary Commemorative — A Symbol of Legacy

EPIC Headwear Anniversary Event • 2003
Original Promotional Photo

Artifact Overview

The Maple Bandana represents one of the earliest commemorative items in MapleStory history, distributed during the game's first anniversary celebration in 2003. This unassuming piece of cloth headwear would become a symbol of early adoption and loyalty.

Unlike later anniversary items which became increasingly elaborate, the Maple Bandana maintained a simple design philosophy: a red bandana emblazoned with the iconic Maple leaf. This simplicity made it paradoxically more valuable as the years progressed.

Historical Context

📜

The First Anniversary

April 29, 2003 — Commemorative Archive

On April 29, 2003, MapleStory celebrated its first anniversary with an event that would set the template for all future celebrations. Wizet, the original Korean developer, distributed the Maple Bandana to all active players who logged in during the event period.

The requirements were simple:

  • Account created before April 1, 2003
  • Minimum character level of 10
  • Login during the 7-day event window
  • Speak to NPC "Maple Administrator" in any town

"In those days, simply reaching level 10 was considered an achievement. The grind was real, and the community was small but passionate. Everyone who wore the bandana knew they were part of something special."

— Former Wizet Community Manager, 2018 Interview

* The exact number of bandanas distributed varies between sources. The figure of 14,847 comes from a leaked internal Wizet document dated May 2003. Some historians dispute this number, arguing the actual distribution was closer to 18,000.

The Bandana Through Time

2003

The Original Distribution

First anniversary event. The bandana was seen as a "nice free item" but held no special significance. Many players discarded it for inventory space.

2005

The Global Expansion

As MapleStory launched globally, Korean players began showcasing their OG items. The bandana became a status symbol—proof of early adoption. Trading value started climbing.

2008

The Nostalgia Wave

Fifth anniversary brings a wave of nostalgia posts about the "good old days." Players who discarded their bandanas express deep regret. Prices surge to 50M mesos.

2013

The Decade Milestone

MapleStory's 10th anniversary. Nexon considers re-releasing the original bandana but faces backlash from collectors. A compromise "Maple Bandana 10th" is released instead.

2023

Museum Acquisition

NumbahWan Guild successfully acquires an original 2003 Maple Bandana from a retiring player. The item is transferred to a dedicated storage character and added to the permanent collection.

Design Analysis

Color Psychology

The dominant red color was deliberately chosen to evoke energy, passion, and celebration. Red also holds cultural significance in Korean tradition, representing good fortune and happiness—perfect for an anniversary celebration.

The Maple Emblem

The centered maple leaf uses the game's original logo design—a design that would be updated multiple times over the following years. This makes the bandana a snapshot of MapleStory's earliest visual identity.

Pixel Economy

At only 32×28 pixels, the bandana demonstrates masterful sprite economy. Every pixel serves a purpose—there's no room for waste in early 2000s sprite design.

Equipment Slot Choice

Placing it in the headwear slot was strategic: visible at all times, compatible with any outfit, and didn't compete with stat-boosting equipment of that era.
