About the Museum

A Sacred Duty to Preserve What Matters Most

In the annals of human achievement, few endeavors rival the cultural significance of digital fashion preservation. The NumbahWan Fashion Museum was established in 2024 following a groundbreaking resolution by the Guild Council, which recognized that certain virtual artifacts had transcended their original purpose to become irreplaceable touchstones of collective memory.

Our climate-controlled galleries maintain a constant temperature of 18.5°C and 45% humidity—conditions determined through years of research to be optimal for the long-term preservation of pixelated materials. Each display case is constructed from museum-grade borosilicate glass with UV-filtering properties, ensuring that these treasures will remain vibrant for generations yet unborn.

"When I first proposed the museum, people laughed. They said, 'Who would pay to see old game items?' But I knew—I knew in my heart—that these artifacts represented something greater. They are monuments to countless hours of human dedication, to friendships forged in virtual worlds, to dreams pursued with pixelated determination."

— RegginA, Founder & Chief Curator

Today, scholars from around the globe visit our institution to study these remarkable specimens. Our research department has published over forty peer-reviewed papers examining the sociocultural implications of digital fashion, and our educational programs have reached thousands of young people eager to understand this pivotal chapter in human history.
