How to Play

Master the fundamentals of NumbahWan TCG

Goal

Reduce your opponent's HP from 30 to 0 using Heroes, Minions, Spells, and strategic plays.

Deck

Build a deck of 30 cards. Max 2 copies of any card (1 for Mythic/Legendary).

Mana

Start with 1 Mana, gain +1 each turn (max 10). Spend Mana to play cards.

Combat

Minions attack enemies. ATK deals damage, DEF absorbs it. SPD determines attack order.

Turn Phases

1. Draw Phase

Draw 1 card from your deck. First player skips draw on turn 1.

2. Mana Phase

Gain +1 max Mana (up to 10). All Mana refreshes.

3. Main Phase

Play cards, use abilities, equip items. No limit on actions if you have Mana.

4. Battle Phase

Command minions to attack. Minions can't attack the turn they're summoned (unless Charge).

5. End Phase

Trigger end-of-turn effects. Pass to opponent.

Keywords

  • Taunt - Enemies must attack this minion first
  • Charge - Can attack immediately when summoned
  • Stealth - Cannot be targeted until it attacks
  • Divine Shield - Blocks the first damage taken
  • Flying - Can only be blocked by other Flying minions
  • Lifesteal - Heals your hero for damage dealt
  • First Strike - Deals damage before the enemy can retaliate
  • Deathrattle - Effect triggers when the minion dies

Card Types

Learn the 6 card types and how to use them

HERO

Heroes are powerful unique characters. Only 1 Hero can be on the field at a time. They have high stats and game-changing abilities. Heroes stay until destroyed.

SPELL

Spells are one-time effects that resolve immediately. They can deal damage, heal allies, draw cards, or create powerful effects. After use, they go to the discard pile.

MINION

Minions are creatures that fight for you. They have ATK (damage), DEF (health), and sometimes SPD (attack order). You can have up to 7 minions on the field.

EQUIPMENT

Equipment cards attach to your Hero, giving permanent stat boosts and special abilities. Only 1 weapon and 1 armor can be equipped at a time.

EVENT

Events create temporary game-wide effects that affect both players. They can shift the battle in dramatic ways and create exciting turning points.

LOCATION

Locations are persistent field effects that benefit you each turn. Only 1 Location can be active. Playing a new Location replaces the old one.

Strategy Guide

Pro tips to improve your gameplay

Mana Curve Basics

A good deck has cards at every mana cost. Aim for:

  • 1-2 Mana: 8-10 cards (early game presence)
  • 3-4 Mana: 8-10 cards (mid game power)
  • 5-6 Mana: 6-8 cards (late game threats)
  • 7+ Mana: 2-4 cards (finishers only)

Board Control

Control the board before going face (attacking hero). Key principles:

  • Trade minions efficiently - use small minions to weaken big threats
  • Save removal spells for high-value targets
  • Position minions carefully - adjacent effects matter!
  • Don't overcommit - save resources for board clears

Card Advantage

The player with more cards usually wins. How to gain advantage:

  • Use 1 card to destroy 2+ enemy cards (value trades)
  • Include card draw in your deck (Shadow Dungeon, Jackpot!)
  • Don't waste cards on low-value plays
  • Heroes like Reggino provide card draw on play

Top 5 Beginner Mistakes

  • 1. Playing on curve blindly - Sometimes holding cards is better
  • 2. Ignoring enemy Taunt - You MUST attack Taunt first
  • 3. Forgetting SPD - Higher SPD attacks first in combat
  • 4. Wasting removal on small minions - Save Thunder Strike for big threats
  • 5. No win condition - Every deck needs a way to close games

Deck Archetypes

Aggro

Fast, cheap minions. Win by turn 6-7. Use Charge minions and burn spells.

Control

Survive early, win late. Heavy removal, board clears, big finishers.

Midrange

Balanced approach. Strong minions at every cost. Flexible gameplay.

Combo

Assemble specific cards for huge turns. Time Warp + burst damage.
