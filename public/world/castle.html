AERIAL VIEW

WORLD OVERVIEW

Castle NumbahWan dominates the landscape like a golden crown on a mountain's brow. Seven stories of weathered stone and enchanted masonry rise from the earth, each floor wider than a city block. The castle's footprint is massive — not just a fortress but a self-contained civilization. Inside the walls, the green canopy of the Monkey Mountain Reserve is clearly visible from above, a living forest ecosystem thriving within medieval stone. Waterfalls cascade down rocky faces, bamboo groves sway in the wind, and if you squint, tiny pixel monkeys dot the canopy like freckles on the earth.

Outside the walls, a deep blue moat circles the entire structure, fed by underground springs. A single drawbridge connects to a cobblestone road that winds through diverse biomes — cherry blossom groves to the east, dark enchanted forests to the north, and mushroom fields glowing faintly to the west. A small village of NPC homes and market stalls clusters near the road, sustained by the guild's commerce. Farmland stretches to the south. On the horizon, a mountain range frames the world, and if you're lucky, you'll spot a dragon silhouette against the setting sun.

EXTERIOR

ARCHITECTURAL NOTES

The castle rises like a golden mountain against the night sky. Built from dark volcanic stone quarried from the northern mountains, every block was laid by the founding members during the First Age of NumbahWan. The architecture is brutalist-medieval — thick walls, narrow arrow slits, and reinforced buttresses — but softened by the signature orange-gold aesthetic the guild is known for. Guild banners bearing the golden "N" emblem hang from every tower, catching the wind like flames.

Seven distinct floors are visible from outside, each marked by a ring of amber-lit windows. The gothic corner towers feature flame beacons that burn eternally — fueled by enchanted oil from the Oracle Tower. A cliffside path spirals up the eastern face for those brave enough to skip the interior stairs. At the very top, the Observatory dome glints under starlight, and on clear nights, you can see the telescope lens reflecting moonbeams. The Sacred Log totem — a relic of the guild's founding meme — stands guard at the main entrance like a wooden sentinel. Bats circle the upper towers at dusk, and an aurora occasionally paints the sky in guild colors.

MATERIALDark volcanic stone from the Northern Quarry, reinforced with dwarven iron beams. Walls are 4 meters thick at the base.
HEIGHTApproximately 85 meters from moat level to the Observatory dome. Each floor averages 10m ceiling height.
DEFENSESArrow slits on every floor, boiling oil channels above gates, enchanted gargoyles that animate during sieges, and the moat (home to territorial pixel fish).
AESTHETICNumbahWan Gold — every banner, crest, and accent uses the guild's signature amber-gold (#e8a922). Even the mortar between stones has a faint golden shimmer.

MAIN GATE

GATE DETAILS

The main gate of Castle NumbahWan is an engineering marvel of the medieval world. A massive stone archway — 12 meters tall — bears the golden "N" crest carved into its keystone, inlaid with actual gold leaf that catches torchlight and makes the entrance glow even at midnight. The half-raised iron portcullis weighs over 3 tons, operated by a chain-and-counterweight system maintained by the castle's engineering corps (really just RegginO and a pulley).

Two guard statues flank the entrance: one holds a sword (representing Offense, or RegginA's philosophy), the other cradles the Sacred Log (representing the guild's sense of humor). The wooden drawbridge spans a glowing blue moat — the water enchanted with bioluminescent algae that makes it shimmer at night. Pixel fish dart beneath the surface, some rumored to be enchanted goldfish that grant wishes (unconfirmed). A notice board titled "Guild Notices" hangs beside the gate, pinned with quest postings, wanted posters, and Karen's weekly meal schedule. Chonk the orange cat is always sleeping somewhere near the gate, unbothered by the traffic of merchants, adventurers, and the occasional stray monkey that wandered down from the mountain reserve.

PORTCULLIS3-ton iron gate, chain-operated. Takes 4 guards to raise manually, or 1 RegginO with enough motivation.

COURTYARD

COURTYARD LORE

The courtyard is the beating heart of Castle NumbahWan — an open-air stone plaza roughly 50 meters across, surrounded on all sides by the castle's towering walls. At its center stands the Fountain of the Golden N: an ornate marble structure where water sprays from a gilded "N" statue, said to have been blessed by the Oracle. Guild members toss coins into the fountain for good luck before boss raids. The water is crystal clear and always warm, heated by pipes running from the basement forges.

The Sacred Banana Tree grows in a protected garden alcove to the east — a living relic from the guild's founding era, its golden fruit glows faintly at night and is said to grant temporary buffs (or at least a good snack). Training dummies made of straw and scrap wood line the western wall for combat practice. A horse stable houses three pixel horses and a hay cart. The blacksmith's outdoor anvil sends sparks into the afternoon air. Children NPCs play tag around the fountain while a monkey from the mountain reserve — they always find a way out — steals bread from an unattended basket. Cobblestone paths radiate outward to every major wing of the castle.

MONKEY MOUNTAIN

THE MOUNTAIN RESERVE — FULL DESCRIPTION

When the founders of NumbahWan designed the castle, they made a decision no other guild had ever attempted: they built a mountain inside the walls. Not a hill. Not a garden. An actual rocky mountain with waterfalls, bamboo forests, cherry blossom trees, hot springs, and — most importantly — monkeys. Lots and lots of monkeys. The Monkey Mountain Reserve occupies the entire northeastern quarter of the castle grounds, rising 40 meters from the courtyard floor to a peak that nearly reaches the fourth-floor windows. It's enclosed by the castle's outer walls but open to the sky, creating a natural amphitheater of stone and green.

The mountain is home to over 200 pixel monkeys of various species — spider monkeys swinging from bamboo, macaques grooming each other on mossy boulders, baby monkeys riding their mothers' backs through the canopy, and the legendary Monkey King who sits on a golden throne-rock at the peak wearing a tiny crown (a gift from Natehouoho himself). A winding cobblestone path leads through the reserve, lined with stone lanterns and orange-gold NWG banners on poles. Guild members walk these paths daily — a knight stops to pet a curious monkey, a mage feeds bananas to a gathering crowd of them, and the Guild Master Natehouoho stands at the scenic overlook with a walking staff, surveying his green domain.

Hot springs bubble up from volcanic vents near the base, their steam mixing with morning mist to create an ethereal atmosphere. A wooden bridge arches over a crystal stream that feeds down from the waterfalls. Bamboo fences mark the trail boundaries (frequently ignored by monkeys). A small Shinto-style shrine sits at the halfway point, where members leave offerings of fruit and coins. Butterflies and pixel birds fill the air. Fireflies emerge at dusk, turning the reserve into a constellation of floating lights. Mushrooms grow on fallen logs, some bioluminescent. The sacred NWG golden "N" emblem is carved into the mountainside, visible from the courtyard — a permanent mark of who this mountain belongs to.

MONKEY POPULATION200+ residents across 5 species. The Monkey King (crowned) sits atop the peak. Baby monkeys are the most mischievous — they steal hats, food, and once stole RegginO's vice master badge.
WATERFALLSThree cascades fed by an underground aquifer. The largest falls 15 meters into a pool where monkeys swim. The water is drinkable and rumored to taste slightly of bananas.

ESCAPE RATE3-5 monkeys escape weekly into the castle proper. They always target the tavern (food), the AFK lounge (snacks), and the library (they eat the bookmarks).
BANANA GROVE47 banana trees planted along the eastern slope. Annual harvest: ~2,000 bananas. 60% eaten by monkeys before picking. The remaining 40% fuels the tavern's famous banana bread.
WALKING PATHS2.3km of cobblestone trails wind through the reserve. Scenic overlooks at 3 elevations. Benches every 200m. The Guild Master's favorite overlook faces west for sunset views.

F1 — MARKET

MARKET DETAILS

The Grand Market occupies the entire western wing of Floor 1 — a vaulted stone hall lined with merchant stalls, each draped in guild-colored cloth. The blacksmith's forge blazes at the far end, hammering enchanted weapons to order. The potion shop bubbles with colorful liquids in glass vials. A dedicated card trading stall displays the rarest NWG cards behind enchanted glass — collectors from other servers travel here just to browse. Food vendors sell everything from roasted boar legs to the tavern's legendary banana bread (made with fruit from the Mountain Reserve). Lanterns hang from chains, casting warm amber light across cobblestone floors. Crates of goods line the walls, stamped with origin marks from across the game world.

The market is always bustling — NPC merchants hawk their wares, guild members haggle over upgrade materials, and at least one monkey (escaped from the reserve) is always spotted stealing fruit from a vendor's display. The NWG banners overhead flutter in the draft from the ventilation shafts. A community board near the entrance tracks daily prices for key commodities. The guild takes a 2% tax on all transactions, funding castle maintenance and the weekly boss raid supplies.

STALLS12 permanent merchant stalls: blacksmith, potions, cards, food, armor, accessories, scrolls, pets, crafting materials, luxury goods, gems, and the "mystery box" stall.
