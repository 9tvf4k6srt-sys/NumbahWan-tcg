# VOICE — PINFORGE / NumbahWan Register

The single source of truth for how we sound. Read this before writing copy in any language. If a draft you produce can't survive the test at the bottom of this file, rewrite it.

---

## Who PINFORGE is, in one breath

A Taipei-rooted boutique wealth advisor. Family office instincts, not bank-marketing instincts. Two principals (CL and HW) who treat the client's money like their own grandparents' money. Conservative by default. Receipts before opinions. Iron-clad before bold.

This is not Bloomberg. Not HSBC. Not Goldman. Not a hedge fund pitching its book. The register is closer to a senior tailor explaining why this fabric and not that one — quiet, certain, with the occasional dry aside.

## The three languages

### English — dry, direct, slightly British in restraint

- Short sentences carry weight. Long sentences earn it by saying something specific.
- First person plural ("we") for the firm. Second person ("you") for the client. Never the royal "I".
- Contractions are fine ("we don't", "it's"). Forced formality reads as AI.
- Dry humor lands. Performative humor doesn't.
- No exclamation marks in body copy. None. They're a tell.

**Example that works** (`invest.html`):
> "We treat your wealth — and your business — like our own. Conservative by default. We only spend a chip when the receipts are iron-clad."

**Why it works:** the em-dash is a real pause, not a flourish. "Spend a chip" is concrete. "Iron-clad" is opinionated. Total length under 30 words.

### 繁中 — Taipei street register, not translation register

- Colloquial. The voice of a senior advisor sitting across the table at a 路邊攤, not the voice of a research note.
- Short, punchy. Uses 你 not 您 unless context demands formality.
- Real Taiwanese phrases: 拽那招、擺著不動、卷宗、白紙黑字、講不出名字就不算. Not 在這個瞬息萬變的市場中.
- Forbidden patterns: 「預約 30 分鐘第一次聊聊」(too literal/translated), 「都在你手上」(too literal). Use 「先約 30 分鐘,坐下來聊」, 「股票你自己抓著」.

**Example that works:**
> "你的錢、你的店,我們當自己的在顧。保守為先。沒有鐵證,一張單都不下。"

**Why it works:** 「自己的在顧」is street-grammar warmth. 「一張單都不下」is trader-floor concrete. Zero translator-tells.

### 日本語 — formal-direct, not flowery

- Polite forms (です/ます) but never honorific stacking.
- Short clauses. Japanese AI translations balloon — ours don't.
- Domain words stay in domain (受注可視性, 鉄証, 法説会).
- No corporate-comms register (〜させていただきます chains). No anime register either.

**Example that works:**
> "お客様の資産も事業も、自分のものとして守ります。保守を旨とし、鉄証なくして一手も打ちません。"

**Why it works:** 自分のものとして carries the family-office stance. 一手 is shogi/go vocabulary that lands in finance.

## Cadence rules (apply across all three languages)

1. **Mix sentence lengths.** Short (under 10 words) and long (over 20). Never three similar-length sentences in a row. This is the single biggest AI tell.
2. **Active voice.** "We cut the position" not "the position was cut."
3. **Concrete over abstract.** "NT$2.16B over the tenor" beats "significant value." A name beats "a major customer."
4. **Specifics earn trust.** Dates, numbers, ticker symbols, named people. Vague language fails. The B-screen on the desk requires named customers for a reason — same rule applies to copy.
5. **One idea per paragraph.** When a paragraph has two ideas, split it. When it has three, kill one.
6. **Kill the wind-up.** Don't say "It's worth noting that…" or "在這個快速變化的市場中…". Just say the thing.
7. **End on the verb when you can.** "We spend a chip when the receipts are iron-clad" beats "When the receipts are iron-clad, we will then consider deploying capital."

## Phrases we own (use these, they carry the brand)

- **"Receipts, not promises."** Headline-grade.
- **"The names rotate. The discipline doesn't."** Closes the framing problem on rotation pages.
- **"Every change is dated and signed."** Audit-trail proof.
- **"Iron-clad."** / **「鐵證」** / **「鉄証」** — the cross-language anchor.
- **"Cheap to fair, increasing moat."** Two-criterion shorthand.
- **"訂單能見度"** — central B-screen label, never translated to "demand visibility" or similar weak English.

## Phrases we never use (pre-commit blocks most of these — see REJECTIONS.md)

EN: delve, tapestry, pivotal, furthermore, state-of-the-art, paradigm shift, in a world where, journey through, testament to, navigate (generic), embark, leverage (verb), elevate, robust, vibrant, showcase (verb in marketing register), craft (verb in marketing register), unleash, unlock potential, comprehensive, holistic, seamlessly, cutting-edge, world-class.

繁中: 卷軸 (in editorial context — game allowlist exempt), 在這個快速變化的市場中, 為您打造, 讓我們一起, 攜手共創, 賦能, 全方位, 一站式, 沉浸式 (unless literal), 解鎖.

日本語: 〜の世界へ, 〜への旅, 〜を解き放つ, 〜のタペストリー, 〜のパラダイム, シナジー (unless gaming).

## How we open

**Bad opening:** "In today's fast-moving AI hardware market, identifying winners requires…"
**Good opening:** "Five names. Six tests. We cut anything that fails one."

**Bad opening:** "我們致力於為客戶打造優質的投資體驗"
**Good opening:** "你的錢、你的店,當自己的在顧。"

The first sentence does work. It does not warm up.

## How we close

The close is operational, not motivational.

- **Bad close:** "Begin your journey with PINFORGE today."
- **Good close:** "先約 30 分鐘,坐下來聊。"
- **Good close:** "Open ledger →"
- **Good close:** "Reply 1, 2, or 3."

Closes either give the next concrete step or stop. They never inspire.

## Pulse-dot kicker pattern (signature)

Live-ops feel beats marketing. Use the pulse-dot kicker on any panel that updates:

> ● 47 rotations since inception · last receipt 2026-05-06 by CL

The pulse dot, the count, the date, the analyst initials — that combination is ours. Don't water it down with "we are constantly updating" filler.

## Tone calibration table

| Situation | Register |
|---|---|
| Hero copy | Confident, brief, opinionated |
| Footer | Quiet, factual |
| Error states | Honest, instructive, no apology theater |
| Caveats | Unflinching. List the limits. Don't soften. |
| Pushback to operator | Direct. "Here's what won't work and why." |
| Numbers / receipts | Mono font, dated, sourced. Never round to "approximately". |
| Pricing | Range or "talk to us". Never aspirational adjectives. |

## The honesty floor (non-negotiable)

When the answer is "we don't know" or "we can't measure that yet", say so. We have already shipped a desk where every receipt is dated, every PT input has a verify_status, every cycle stage flag is honest about being draft. Copy must match that bar. **A confident-sounding sentence built on a draft input is a lie.** Mark it draft, or rewrite around it.

## The test

Before shipping any copy, ask:

1. Could a senior PINFORGE partner read this aloud at a client meeting in Taipei without flinching?
2. Are any of the rejected phrases above present?
3. Are three similar-length sentences in a row?
4. Are there exclamation marks in body copy?
5. Does the first sentence do work, or warm up?
6. Are claims backed by dates, names, numbers?
7. If you remove every adjective, does the meaning survive?

If you can't answer yes / no / no / no / yes / yes / yes — rewrite.

## What this voice is NOT

- Not Bloomberg ("market participants are positioning…").
- Not WeWork ("elevate your portfolio's potential").
- Not anime / kawaii.
- Not professor ("it should be noted that…").
- Not hedge fund swagger ("our edge is…").
- Not LinkedIn motivational ("your journey starts here").

If a draft drifts toward any of those, it has drifted out of voice.
