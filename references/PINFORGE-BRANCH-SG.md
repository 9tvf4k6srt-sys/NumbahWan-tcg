# PINFORGE Branch — Singapore (SG)

Sister desk to Taipei. Same firm, same brand marks, **deliberately different design language** so each branch reads as its city, not as a copy-paste.

This file is the canonical visual lock for every PINFORGE-Singapore image. Every SG generation must cite both this file AND `references/PINFORGE-VISUAL-LOCK.md`.

---

## Why a different DNA

Taipei is scholar-luxe — Vincent Van Duysen warmth × Joseph Dirand sharpness × Axel Vervoordt patina. Sumi ink, oxidized brass, lime plaster. **Cold-temperate / paper-toned**.

Singapore is one degree from the equator. Year-round 28–32 °C, 80% humidity, monsoon afternoon storms, harsh equatorial light. The Taipei DNA — raw plaster, walnut, heavy textiles — would warp, mildew, and feel wrong in a tropical tower. The branch must answer to its climate.

**SG designer DNA**: Kerry Hill (rigorous tropical modernism) × Ole Scheeren (vertical-city geometry) × Studio Tonton / Soo Chan (SG-rooted material restraint) × WOHA (biophilic / louvered tropical).

This is **not** Aman Tropical Resort. Not "luxury hotel lobby". Not Marina Bay tourist gloss.

## Locked architecture — SG branch

- Singapore CBD tower, **mid-floor 22–35F realistic range** (lower than Taipei because SG towers cluster denser)
- Located feel: Tanjong Pagar / Raffles Place / Shenton Way district
- 3.2 m ceiling (slightly lower than Taipei's 3.6 m — denser tower stock)
- **Full-height curtain-wall glazing** with **deep external louver fins** or **operable timber louvers** inside (Kerry Hill / WOHA tropical-shading vocabulary)
- Polished concrete floor with honed limestone insets (NOT travertine — that's Taipei)
- **Burmese teak** screen wall (NOT walnut — that's Taipei)
- **Black powder-coated steel** detailing (NOT oxidized brass on furniture — brass reserved for the emblem only)
- Raw board-formed concrete back wall with cast-brass PINFORGE emblem inset (same emblem, same 30–40cm size, same off-center placement as Taipei)
- One climate-correct plant: **single tembusu sapling** in a board-formed concrete planter, OR a low **monstera** under the louvers — never a fiddle-leaf fig (cliché)

## Locked camera language — SG branch

Same equipment grammar as Taipei (we're one firm) but tuned for tropical light:

- Phase One IQ4 medium-format aesthetic, OR Leica Q3 35mm
- f/4–f/5.6, ISO 200
- **Equatorial light is harder than Taipei** — direct sun is brutal at noon, beautiful at 4–6pm. Shoot side-light from louvers (slatted shadow patterns on the floor).
- **Monsoon afternoon variant**: heavy rain on the glass, grey-green light, interior practicals warmer (2700K) against the cool exterior
- Portra 400 grain (added in code, not prompt)
- Asymmetric composition, off-center
- Single hand-applied imperfection per frame: a damp umbrella by the door, a sweating glass of water leaving a ring, a kopi cup (not coffee — kopi-O at the desk)

## Brand consistency rules across branches

What stays **identical** across Taipei and Singapore:
- The cast-brass PINFORGE emblem (twin pillars + N-wedge), 30–40cm, off-center, on a raw masonry back wall
- The cinnabar wax-seal stamp on debossed walnut (Taipei) / teak (SG) folios
- The 繁體 "品峰" engraving (Singapore office still uses 繁體 because firm is Taipei-headquartered, not 简体)
- Camera grammar (Phase One / Leica Q3, f/4, Portra 400 grain)
- The **single hand-applied imperfection** rule

What **changes** between branches:
- Floor material (travertine → polished concrete)
- Wood species (walnut → Burmese teak)
- Back wall (raw lime plaster → board-formed concrete)
- Window grammar (curtain wall → curtain wall **with louver shading**)
- Climate cues (dry warm light → wet equatorial light, monsoon variant)
- Local props (Buddha-pine 真柏 → tembusu sapling; tea ceremony set → kopi cup)
- Skyline ID (Taipei 101 side-on → Tanjong Pagar cluster, no MBS)
